**TAKEAWAYS**
- Education networks are formed by <Underlining color={Theme.colors.secondary} hoverColor={Theme.colors.secondary}>different actors whith different interests</Underlining> in education and education reform.

- Teacher function as <Underlining color={Theme.colors.secondary} hoverColor={Theme.colors.secondary}>relays of education networks</Underlining>, transporting the global ideas to local levels.

- Those actors who promote an education reform are often <Underlining color={Theme.colors.secondary} hoverColor={Theme.colors.secondary}>advocating increased use of edtech</Underlining>.

- Neoliberal ideals are implemented as <Underlining color={Theme.colors.secondary} hoverColor={Theme.colors.secondary}>technologies that aim at performance and standardization</Underlining>, often over-simplifying the social realities at schools.

- Some teachers experience a <Underlining color={Theme.colors.secondary} hoverColor={Theme.colors.secondary}>tension between their performative role and their self-image</Underlining>, which leads to different coping mechanisms.
